AAbout Me

Hello. I am Miguel Zapata, an aspiring Software Developer from the University of Minnesota with deep interest in both hardware and software. I’ve was fascinated by how computers work on the inside. My hands on experience with deceive by taking apart old devices, doing repairs, and understanding the engineering behind the technology has led my to explore computer engineering then later computer science. My journey to graduation isn’t always been straightforward. I didn’t immediately click with computer science as I struggled at first, but overtime I found my place in areas where software and hardware overlap. I enjoy troubleshooting, using tools like terminals and debuggers, and finding ways to build programs that can run smoothly even on limited hardware. I am a first-generation college graduate after being a commuter student for years. And all of that shapes in the way I learn, work, and collaborate.

As a first-generation student and someone who’s still finding their way, I’ve learned to move forward at my own pace. I care about growth, not the kind that looks good on paper. For me it’s about staying grounded, learning through effort, holding onto what matters as I adapt to new challenges.

Curiosity
